About Me

Bridging the gap between clinical neurology and computational neuroscience

{{ about.bio }}

My name is Tatiana Ozhgibesova, and I am a medical doctor specializing in neurology with a deep commitment to understanding the human brain—its structures, its functions, and its remarkable ability to adapt. Over the years, I have combined my clinical expertise with an expanding skill set in computational neuroscience, data science, and neurotechnology, creating a unique professional profile that bridges medicine, research, and technology.

I began my career in clinical neurology, where I gained extensive experience diagnosing and treating neurological disorders, as well as interpreting clinical EEG. In this role, I learned to appreciate both the complexity and the resilience of the nervous system. My work with patients gave me a direct understanding of the human side of neuroscience—the urgent need for accurate diagnosis, effective treatment, and compassionate care. However, I also developed a strong curiosity about the underlying mechanisms of brain function, and I became increasingly motivated to explore neuroscience from a research and computational perspective.

This drive led me to Germany, where I completed a neuroscience program in Magdeburg. Immersed in a multidisciplinary academic environment, I explored topics ranging from visual perception and synaptic plasticity to network oscillations and neurodegenerative processes. I participated in lectures and research training covering areas such as:

  • Neurotrophins in survival, differentiation, and plasticity
  • Cellular mechanisms of network oscillations
  • Dendritic spine dynamics
  • Neurodegeneration and neuroprotection
  • Genetic programs in development and neurodevelopmental disorders

These experiences strengthened my understanding of both classical neurotransmission and modern molecular neuroscience, while also reinforcing the need for quantitative, computational approaches in brain research.

Parallel to my academic training, I began building strong data science skills. I became proficient in Python and key libraries such as NumPy, Pandas, and Matplotlib for statistical analysis, visualization, and computational modeling. My work has included:

  • Designing and implementing data processing pipelines for neuroscience experiments
  • Performing statistical analyses including bootstrapping, permutation tests, and linear discriminant analysis (LDA)
  • Fitting mathematical models (linear, exponential) to experimental data to uncover trends and relationships
  • Visualizing complex datasets to make patterns interpretable for both scientists and clinicians

My technical expertise allows me to bridge the gap between traditional clinical neurology and modern computational neuroscience. I am especially passionate about projects that combine brain-computer interfaces (BCI), cognitive technology, and machine learning—fields that have the potential to revolutionize diagnostics, rehabilitation, and everyday human-computer interaction.

In addition to my academic and technical work, I have been committed to expanding my language skills and adapting to international collaboration. Originally from Russia, I am fluent in Russian, have conversational proficiency in English, and am currently studying German (A2 level) to fully integrate into the German medical and research community. I believe that science thrives when communication barriers are minimized, and learning languages has not only opened doors professionally but also enriched my ability to connect with people from different backgrounds.

One of my greatest strengths is the ability to connect multiple disciplines into a coherent approach to problem-solving. In medicine, we often deal with uncertainty—symptoms can be ambiguous, test results can be inconclusive, and treatment effects can vary widely between patients. Similarly, in neuroscience research, experimental data is rarely perfect; noise, variability, and missing information are constant challenges. My experience in both the clinical and computational worlds has taught me to embrace this uncertainty while applying rigorous analytical methods to extract meaningful conclusions.

Beyond professional achievements, I am deeply motivated by a vision: to create tools, therapies, and systems that improve quality of life for patients with neurological conditions. I see immense potential in technologies that merge neuroscience with data-driven insights, from adaptive BCIs that restore lost communication abilities to AI-assisted EEG interpretation tools that make diagnosis faster and more accurate.

To achieve this vision, I continuously seek opportunities to collaborate with research labs, startups, and industry leaders in neurotechnology. I am particularly interested in visual neuroscience projects, where understanding the brain's processing of visual information could lead to innovations in both medical treatment and augmented reality systems.

Professional Qualities

Some of my most valuable professional qualities include:

  • Clinical Expertise: Years of hands-on experience in neurology, EEG interpretation, and patient care
  • Scientific Rigor: Trained in advanced neuroscience concepts and experimental methods
  • Computational Skills: Proficiency in Python, data analysis, visualization, and mathematical modeling
  • Adaptability: Ability to work across disciplines, languages, and cultures
  • Curiosity & Lifelong Learning: Constantly seeking new knowledge in neuroscience, medicine, and technology

I believe the future of neurology will be shaped by integration—integration of clinical insight with computational modeling, of patient needs with technological capabilities, and of fundamental neuroscience with applied innovation. My career path reflects this philosophy: moving from bedside diagnosis to data-driven research, from traditional EEG readings to advanced computational analysis, from isolated expertise to multidisciplinary problem-solving.

When I am not working on academic or professional projects, I enjoy exploring scientific literature, practicing German, and keeping up with emerging developments in AI and neuroscience. I also value creativity outside of science—whether it's photography, visual design, or creative writing—because I believe that innovation in science often comes from unexpected connections and perspectives.

Future Goals

Looking forward, my professional goals include:

  • Joining a research or industry team focused on brain-computer interfaces or cognitive technologies
  • Contributing to translational neuroscience that moves discoveries from the lab into clinical practice
  • Bridging the medical and computational worlds through projects that directly benefit patients
  • Continuing medical practice in Germany while integrating modern neurotechnology into diagnostic and treatment workflows

I welcome collaborations with researchers, developers, and healthcare innovators who share the belief that understanding the brain is the first step to improving human life. Whether in a research lab, a hospital, or a tech startup, I aim to bring my combined expertise in neurology, neuroscience, and data science to projects that truly matter.

In summary, I am Tatiana Ozhgibesova—neurologist, neuroscientist, and data-driven problem solver. My journey blends clinical medicine with computational analysis, local patient care with global research collaboration, and scientific curiosity with a clear mission: to unlock the brain's secrets for the benefit of patients and society.
